# Roadmap

This roadmap assumes we are building an original early learning app inspired by Khan Academy Kids patterns, but optimized around our parent-curated dashboard model.

## 1. Product Phases

### Phase 0: Foundation and validation

Goals:

- confirm target age range,
- confirm visual direction,
- confirm first 6-8 packs,
- confirm dashboard selection model,
- confirm audio and stimulation rules.

Deliverables:

- product blueprint,
- content taxonomy,
- design system,
- wireframes,
- technical architecture draft.

### Phase 1: MVP

Goals:

- launch the smallest lovable version of the product.

Required features:

- adult account,
- child profiles,
- avatar selection,
- parent gate,
- focus mode,
- shelf mode,
- library with filters,
- pin to dashboard,
- reorder dashboard,
- books with narration,
- 6-8 core packs,
- one simple create tool,
- sound settings,
- progress states,
- offline-ready subset.

Recommended MVP packs:

- First Words
- Animals
- Colors and Shapes
- Count to 5
- Faces and Feelings
- Brush Teeth
- Story Listening
- Color and Paint

### Phase 2: Stronger learning and calm controls

Goals:

- deepen educational usefulness,
- improve parent trust,
- improve replay value.

Features:

- more books,
- reading levels or age levels,
- recommendation engine,
- low-stimulation mode,
- reduced motion,
- routine packs,
- calm corner,
- off-screen prompt system,
- simple parent progress summaries.

### Phase 3: Content breadth and stronger differentiation

Goals:

- build the large internal library without cluttering the child home screen.

Features:

- 20-30 packs,
- story retell tools,
- voice recording gallery,
- shelf rotation,
- timed availability for packs,
- favorites,
- seasonal pack framework,
- multilingual support planning.

### Phase 4: Adult power tools

Goals:

- support schools, therapists, and advanced parent use.

Features:

- assignment flows,
- pack sharing,
- grouped playlists,
- standards mapping,
- teacher or therapist notes,
- richer reports,
- exportable usage summaries.

## 5. Risks

### Risk: too much breadth too early

Mitigation:

- launch with packs,
- reuse interaction templates,
- prioritize quality over quantity.

### Risk: dashboard becomes cluttered

Mitigation:

- default to shelf mode with a small visible limit,
- keep full library behind a parent gate,
- support one-item focus mode.

### Risk: app feels derivative

Mitigation:

- original visual identity,
- different dashboard philosophy,
- stronger routine and off-screen layers,
- distinct guide system.

### Risk: audio and motion overstimulation

Mitigation:

- calm-first design system,
- low-stimulation mode,
- separate audio controls,
- reduced motion support.

## 6. Definition of MVP Success

MVP succeeds if:

- parents can create a child profile quickly,
- parents can pin and reorder dashboard items easily,
- children can open and replay selected content without confusion,
- the home screen stays calm even as the library grows,
- at least 6-8 packs feel polished and reusable,
- books, vocabulary, and routines already demonstrate whole-child value.
